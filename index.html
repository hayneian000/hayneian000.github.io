About Me:

I'm a Software Developer and a proud student at the University of Utah, majoring in both Software Development and History. My anticipated graduation is in 2024, and I've been joyfully developing my skillset and learning how to create and improve software systems that solve real-world problems.

My passion for development began at a young age when I realized the power and potential of software in shaping our future. The thrill of bringing a concept to life through lines of code, the sense of achievement that comes from troubleshooting a persistent bug, and the gratification of seeing a project through to completion, all fuel my love for this field.

I deeply value the intersection of technology and humanities, which is why I chose to also major in history. The past informs the present, and by understanding the evolution of societies, technologies, and ideas, I aim to build software solutions that are not only technologically advanced but are also able to navigate societal nuances.

When I'm not engrossed in my studies or immersed in a coding project, you can usually find me outside. I have an innate love for nature and adventure that often draws me to the breathtaking landscapes of Utah. I spend my free time hiking, hunting, and woodworking, and I find these activities to be both meditative and rewarding. Each of these hobbies requires precision, patience, and an understanding of the natural world, much like software development.

I'm also passionate about physical health and fitness, with weightlifting being one of my favorite forms of exercise. The discipline and rigor required to progress in weightlifting translate well into my approach to software development. The continuous pursuit of self-improvement, the focus on achieving specific goals, and the resilience needed to overcome challenges are attributes I carry from the gym to the keyboard.
